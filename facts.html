Key Facts

Al Gore won the popular vote, but lost the election to George W. Bush. In one of the most contested elections in modern history, the U.S. Supreme Court stopped the Florida recount of ballots and gave Bush the state’s 25 electoral votes, giving him a total 271 compared to Gore’s 255.


James Monroe received every electoral vote but one in 1801. A New Hampshire delegate wanted George Washington to be the only president elected unanimously.


The United States is ranked 139th out of 172 countries in voter participation.


Jimmy Carter was the first president born in a hospital.
